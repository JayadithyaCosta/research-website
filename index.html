-ABSTRACT-

This research aims to address the challenges faced by visually impaired individuals by developing a mobile application that provides assistance in their daily tasks. Traditional and modern assistive devices often fall short due to usability, data accuracy, and cost limitations. The proposed mobile application eliminates the need for external devices and utilizes the smartphone's camera for data capture. It incorporates an intelligent voice assistant to guide users in navigation, object identification, text and currency reading, and weather forecasting. Additionally, the application includes an Ishihara test to help colorblind individuals identify their specific color blindness category. The objective is to enhance data processing speed, improve accuracy, and increase the overall effectiveness of the application.
